# Wealth tax

In the folder `annexes/wealth/`, write the appendix on Wealth Taxation. Every assertion should be properly justified, citing academic papers when possible. The appendix should have the following structure:
[x] 1. Proposal
One-off wealth tax with low rates, adapting the Californian model and Pisani-Ferry & Mahfouz (2023) proposal. Based on individual wealth on Jan 1, 2027.
Schedule: 3% > 1M, 7.5% > 10M, 15% > 100M payable over thirty years, corresponding to 0.1% > 1M, 0.25% > 10M, 0.5% > 100M each year.
Each year, the taxpayer must pay at least 1/30th of their initial tax liability, adjusted for inflation and a deferral penalty (3%/year for the tax >1M, 5% > 10M, 7% > 100M - aligned with returns on capital at different wealth values, cf. World Inequality Report), or the yearly tax schedule applied to their current wealth. The tax ceases to be owed after 30 years or when a taxpayer pays their remaining tax liability.
The administration allows taxpayers to pay with non-cash liquid assets such as shares in public companies. On a case-by-case basis, the administration can allow taxpayers to pay with illiquid assets (such as shares in non-listed companies) to the extent the taxpayer's liability exceeds their liquid assets.
Deferral Accounts: Unless they pay their entire tax liability in the first year, taxpayers must set a Deferral Account (a contract with the State) whereby they attach assets; the State recovers the taxpayer's liability when the taxpayer dies or moves outside the EU; and the DA is closed only when the tax is paid in full.
A missing payment entails a penalty equal to the inflation + 10%/year, eventually recovered when the sale/dividend of assets in the DA realize.

[x] 2. Revenue estimates
Using https://wid.world/world-wealth-tax-simulator/, make a table with each EU country and EU total as rows, and as columns, yearly revenue estimate from (a) a .1% tax over 1M, (b) .25% > 10M, (c) .5% > 100M, (d) 2% > 1G, a+b+c (central scenario), c+d. Assume 15% depreciation and 20% tax evasion.

[x] 3. Interaction with existing taxes
List all EU countries which currently have a wealth tax, the tax schedule, tax base, and tax revenue from each of these taxes. Explain that the proposed new tax would be additional (not substitute).
